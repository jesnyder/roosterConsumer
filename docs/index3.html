The Sun Never Sets on a World Spinning with hMSC Advanced Therapeutics

Our regenerative medicine community built a global, coordinated effort to translate mesenchymal stromal cell (MSC) research to efficacious therapies. The U.S. National Library of Medicine database, ClinicalTrials.gov indexed over 1416 clinical trials since 1995 that use an MSC intervention. These trials enrolled more than 65,000 participants to treat dozens of conditions, which includes arthritis, multiple sclerosis, amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), and COVID-19. This body of work is your resource. Beyond lists and maps, navigate through our community using this interactive map. Trials are grouped based on the MSC source, which differentiates autologous, bespoke therapeutic manufacturing from allogeneic, batch-produced processes.

To make reliable therapies, the MSC source must be standardized. There has been a historic choice between allogeneic and autologous. Patients donate their own cellular material for autologous therapies, which derisks immune response, but adds lead time to the preparation of the therapy and unknowns to the reliability of the cells. Allogeneic MSCs separate therapeutic manufacture from medical treatment for improved quality control and off-the-shelf accessibility.

Explore the interactive map of trials grouped by MSC source: allogenic (orange), autologous (dark purple), both (light blue), or undeclared (gray). A trial can only belong to one group. If a trial has multiple study sites, it will appear multiple times on the map. Cells pass analytically testing qualified, ready off-the-shelf, and distributed globally for comparative studies. Click on the circular marker to see the trial title, location name, number enrolled, and a link to more information. The radius of the circular marker reflects the number enrolled. Larger circles enrolled more participants. Use the +/- in the upper left hand corner of the map or your mouse scroll wheel to zoom in and out of the map. Use the timeline along the bottom edge to animate the map. Trials appear according to their start date. The button on the top right corner of the map allows you to prescriptively show or hide some of the groups.

The code to make the map is open source, free, and available from github .
